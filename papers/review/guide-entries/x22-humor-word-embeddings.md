**Earlier audience-modeling anchors:** [**Humor in Word Embeddings: Cockamamie Gobbledegook for Nincompoops**](https://proceedings.mlr.press/v97/gultchin19a.html) (Gultchin et al., ICML 2019) `peer-reviewed` `dataset` models individual single-word humor preferences as vectors and finds demographic preference clusters using a 120K-word crowdsourcing study. [**Eigentaste / Jester**](https://eigentaste.berkeley.edu/dataset/) (Goldberg et al., *Information Retrieval* 2001) `peer-reviewed` `dataset` supplies the older recommender-system foundation: its successive releases now contain 6.5M anonymous ratings, though over only 100–158 culturally dated jokes. Together they make audience heterogeneity an estimable object rather than a caveat paragraph.
