**Dyadic laughter and participant perception:** [**Introducing MULAI: A Multimodal Database of Laughter during Dyadic Interactions**](https://aclanthology.org/2020.lrec-1.534/) (Jansen, Truong, Nazareth & Heylen, LREC 2020) `peer-reviewed` `dataset` records 2 hours 14 minutes of human–human interaction with 601 laughs, 168 speech-laughs, and 538 respiration events. Participants rated how humorous they and their partners were, alongside personality and physiological measures. In this sample, laugh duration, pitch, and intensity did not track self-perceived humorousness, while some acoustic properties tracked the partner's perception — direct evidence that laughter production, self-assessment, and interlocutor uptake are different variables. Access requires an individual license from the authors.
