**Real-world laughter tasks for LLMs:** [**SMILE-Next: Teaching Large Language Models to Detect, Classify, and Reason about Laughter**](https://aclanthology.org/2026.acl-long.2023/) (Lee et al., ACL 2026) `peer-reviewed` `dataset` `benchmark` builds question–answer tasks for laughter detection, polite/satirical/mirthful type classification, and explanation from textualized multimodal cues in public real-world videos. It also proposes laughter-specific self-instruction and a Mixture-of-Laugh-Experts model. SMILE-Next broadens evaluation beyond “is there laughter?”, but its public-video sampling, synthetic instruction construction, and textualized cues make it a benchmark of modeled laughter understanding, not a substitute for observing spontaneous participant uptake.
