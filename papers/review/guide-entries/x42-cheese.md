**Smiling, uptake, and failed humor:** [**“Cheese!”: a Corpus of Face-to-face French Interactions**](https://aclanthology.org/2020.lrec-1.59/) (Priego-Valverde, Bigi & Amoyal, LREC 2020) `peer-reviewed` `dataset` contains eleven roughly 15-minute audiovisual conversations between 22 French-speaking friends. Its annotation scheme covers speech, smiles, humorous sequences, and positive or negative reactions, enabling analysis of whether smiling frames humor and how recipients display success or failure. At publication, five interactions had speech and transcription enrichment, but only two had the manual smile and humor annotations used in the exploratory analysis. Reduced media were openly downloadable and the full corpus was available through ORTOLANG on request, so it is a rich but small and partly annotated resource rather than a ready-made large benchmark.
