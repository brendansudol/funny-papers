**Memes as conversational actions:** [**Memes-as-Replies: Can Models Select Humorous Manga Panel Responses?**](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.15842) (Kohita & Yoshioka, 2026) `preprint` `dataset` `benchmark` introduces **MaMe-Re**, pairing 250 synthetic social posts with 400 openly licensed Japanese manga panels to form 100,000 context–reply pairs. Five binary judgments per pair yield 500,000 annotations from 2,325 unique crowdworkers. Overall agreement is extremely low (Fleiss' κ=−.022), though substantial subsets reach high consensus; models struggle to distinguish subtly different witty replies, and adding images does not improve selection. It is a valuable operationalization of humor as context-dependent reply choice, but the contexts are synthetic and the paper's dataset URL was still a placeholder when checked, so the release is not yet independently obtainable.
